01 / About

Product leadership grounded in execution, systems thinking, and measurable business outcomes.

Technical Product Manager and AI product leader with 6+ years spanning software engineering, enterprise platforms, healthcare AI, and product strategy. I translate ambiguous operational problems into shipped products, measurable adoption, and executive-ready roadmaps.

I started in engineering at Citibank, building internal platforms that supported high-volume transaction flows, release pipelines, and operational resilience. That foundation still shapes how I lead product: ambitious strategy matters, but it has to survive architecture reviews, delivery tradeoffs, and adoption reality.

Since moving into product, I have led enterprise platform, workflow automation, and healthcare AI initiatives while strengthening the strategy layer through my MBA at UW Foster. The through-line across each role is consistent: identify where a workflow breaks down, align the right stakeholders, and ship a product that meaningfully changes how the work gets done.

Adoption is the real launch metric

I optimize for behavior change, not just delivery. If users do not adopt the workflow, the product strategy is still incomplete.

Technical depth improves product judgment

My engineering background helps me challenge assumptions early, scope responsibly, and align with teams building under real constraints.

Metrics should sharpen prioritization

I prefer operating metrics that change decisions: adoption, throughput, cycle time, revenue impact, and user trust, not vanity reporting.

AI products need operational rigor

In regulated and workflow-heavy environments, AI only works when evaluation, fallback logic, and stakeholder trust are designed in from day one.
